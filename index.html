I quit tech
to farm.

One farmer for one season testing everything on half an acre with all data shared publicly.

Farm Protocols is an 8-month regenerative farming pilot testing whether soil amendments produce measurably more nutritious food. All data, lab results, and field observations are published in real time as the season unfolds 🌱.

Today's produce is measurably less nutritious than it was 50 years ago, this is one small attempt to see how we can close the gap.

Currently: tomatoes are in the ground, baseline soil tests are back, and the first Brix readings are coming up once fruit sets.

The Experiment Three zones, one question
ZONE 01
No additional input
This soil carries 25 years of farmer Ethel's amendment history. It's the starting point for this experiment.
ZONE 02
Compost added
Does adding horse manure compost alone move nutrient density at all within a single season?
ZONE 03
Compost + biochar
Same compost as Zone 2 but with biochar mixed in. Biochar is supposed to help plants take more nutrients out of the soil, can it help in one season?

This experiment runs on half an acre. The other two acres of the 2.5-acre farmable area are where I'm learning to farm alongside Ethel, an experienced farmer who is teaching me to grow vegetables and fruit for us and the community.
